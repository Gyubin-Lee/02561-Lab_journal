Comparison of Projection Shadows and Shadow Mapping

Projection Shadows

  • Advantages:
    • Simplicity: Easy to implement, often just involves a matrix projection onto a plane.
    • Performance: Fast, as it typically requires fewer per-pixel operations.
    • No Extra Memory: Does not necessarily require additional buffers like textures.
  • Disadvantages:
    • Limited Generality: Works best for flat receiving surfaces; not suitable for complex geometry.
    • No Self-Shadowing: Cannot accurately depict objects casting shadows onto themselves.
    • Lack of Realism: Complex lighting and multiple objects are not well represented.

Shadow Mapping

  • Advantages:
    • Flexibility: Works on arbitrary surfaces, not limited to planes.
    • Self-Shadowing: Objects can cast shadows onto themselves and others, adding realism.
    • Scalability: One shadow map can handle multiple objects and complex scenes.
  • Disadvantages:
    • Memory Usage: Requires additional texture buffers, increasing memory consumption.
    • Artifacts: Susceptible to aliasing, "acne," and other visual issues without careful tweaking.
    • Performance Overhead: Involves extra rendering passes and texture lookups.
